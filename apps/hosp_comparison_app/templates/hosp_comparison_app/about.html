About the Data

Prior to 2019, hospital chargemasters were generally closely held, and used primarily for negotiating effective payment rates (also called "allowed" charges) with insurance carriers. Typically the only circumstances in which a patient would directly interact with a chargemaster is if they were both uninsured and wealthy, or if they wished to pay in cash. Otherwise, patient payments were routed through an insurance carrier, which itself paid the discounted allowed charges.

Effective January 2019, CMS now requires Hospitals to post their chargemasters online, along with "standard" charges billed for each MS-DRG. This change was made in order to make an incremental step towards price transparency in an effort to contain rising medical costs.

This site is an effort to centralize the data available into a single platform, and further the push towards pricing transparency.

NOTE: The charges represented on this site are BILLED charges, which often do not represent the actual out-of-pocket cost for patients. These prices are typically only applicable in cases where the patient is uninsured or wishes to pay in cash rather than use insurance. Even in those cases, hospitals are often willing to negotiate discounted pricing in case of inability to pay. Contact your insurance provider if you are seeking to determine your out-of-pocket cost.
